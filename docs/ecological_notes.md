The APA da cafuringa region, just north of Brazil's federal district is a highly uneven ecological region from the ecological and geomorpholocical point of view, and may act as a species barrier for many species. At the same time, it is also plausible that it may act as a hindrance to botanical survey's due to the logistical difficulty of collecting in the region.

A primary exploratory anlaysis revealed that the species distribution is clearly disjunct. Most points area concetrated in the lower wester distribution of the species, while the lower latitude portion has only three scattered points. This may be interpreted as an uneven sampling effort, while at the same time it may also be interpreted as an environmental constraint. Further development in this projetc will aim exactly at this discussion.
